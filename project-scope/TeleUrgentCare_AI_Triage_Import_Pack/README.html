TeleUrgentCare AI Import Pack
2
PurposeImport-ready normalized build pack for an AI-guided asynchronous tele-urgent care platform. This workbook includes the approved symptom catalog, branching question bank, red-flag engine, photo rules, summary mapping, priority rules, and validation checks.
3
Clinical Safety PositionAI must not diagnose or treat the patient directly. AI may only collect history, detect danger, require photos, and summarize for physician review.
4
Hard-stop red flagsThe platform must stop progression for emergency patterns such as severe chest pain, trouble breathing, stroke-like symptoms, loss of consciousness, severe bleeding, severe abdominal pain, severe allergic reaction, suicidal thoughts, sudden paralysis, severe head injury, worst headache of life, inability to swallow liquids, vomiting blood, blood/black stool, cough with blood, and pregnancy-related pain/bleeding danger patterns.
5
Urgent same-day reviewCases that are not full hard stops but still higher-risk should be escalated to the top of the physician queue. Examples: pregnancy with fever or pelvic symptoms, contact lens wearer with red eye, urinary symptoms with fever or flank pain, leg pain/swelling after travel, rash with fever, and possible mania.
6
How to use1) Load symptom_catalog into your master symptom table. 2) Load question_bank into your rules engine. 3) Evaluate red_flag_rules continuously during intake and again before submission. 4) Enforce photo_rules before submission. 5) Use summary_map for physician summary and SOAP-note generation. 6) Use validation to confirm coverage before release.
7
Key implementation ruleAny hard-stop rule must immediately terminate the asynchronous consultation and show the emergency message. Do not allow the patient to 'continue anyway'.
8
Emergency messageYour symptoms may indicate a medical emergency. Please go to the nearest emergency room or call emergency services immediately. This platform cannot safely manage your condition.
9
Note on duplicates in source documentSome symptom labels appear in more than one category (for example runny nose and sneezing). Canonical keys are normalized in symptom_catalog, and source aliases are listed in symptom_aliases.
